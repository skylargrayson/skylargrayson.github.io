I like to talk
about science

Teaching has always been a passion of mine, and I recently
discovered I can explore that passion on social media.

I started on TikTok in February 2022, making videos about astronomy fun facts, life as a grad student, and really whatever came to mind. What began as a creative outlet grew into a platform with over 70 thousand followers, and hundreds of videos on topics ranging from explanations of special relativity, to bad science puns, to the frustrations of programming, to JWST updates.

I decided a couple months after the surprising growth of my TikTok channel to expand to Twitter and Youtube. On my Youtube channel, Space According To Skylar, I repost TikToks and also create longer form videos about life as an astrophysicist, science fiction movies, and general astrophysics topics.

This sort of outreach has been a surprising addition to my life this year, but I'm quickly learning just how much I love it. Beyond allowing me to be creative, the support of the community of people who have found their interest in space rekindled by my videos has been incredible. I hope to continue to grow on all my platforms, sharing my unique form of relatable science communication with people of all ages and backgrounds.
